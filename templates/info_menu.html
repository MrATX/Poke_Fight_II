Welcome to Poke Fight II

Thank you for playing my game! It's a Flask app with mostly JavaScript backend to create a simple GUI-based Pokemon fighting game. Disclaimer: I take no credit for inventing any characters or principal mechanics featured in the game. Setting up a match


To begin a match of Poke Fight II, navigate to the 'Play' page via the link in the top-left corner, then click the button 'Play a Match'

You will then be prompted to choose the settings for the match; Number of Players (only two player local at this time), the Number of Pokemon that each player will have, and the Weight Class of the Pokemon. The Weight Classes are general ranges of the overall strength of the various Pokemon in an attempt to balance the gameplay independent of a leveling system.

Each player will then enter their name and select the respective number of Pokemon from the chosen Weight Class. Once rosters are chosen, you may begin the match.

Choosing Pokemon


This game features nearly every single Pokemon from all 8 generations and the Weight Classes are independent of generation. To explore the Pokemon available and their stats, please check out the Pokedex via its link in the top-left corner. Be sure to make use of the filters by clicking the 'Show Filters' button just below the top Nav Bar.

Combat Mechanics


At the start of a match, each player will select their first Pokemon to send out and the Pokemon with the highest SPD goes first.
The match will proceed with alternating turns for each player. On each turn, you may use a Pokemon's Regular Attack, their Special Attack, or swap them for another Pokemon.

Regular Attack uses the attacking Pokemon's ATK stat and the defending Pokemon's DEF stat. Special Attacks use SP ATK and SP DEF. All attacks have a chance to miss and that chance is higher or lower based on the difference in SPD between Pokemon. If the attacking Pokemon is faster than the defender then they will have a lower chance of missing the attack. If the attacking Pokemon is slower, the chance of missing is higher.

All Attacks also have an effectiveness level which is determined by the interaction of Pokemon types of the attacking and defending Pokemon. Dual-type Pokemon may use two types of attacks for both Regular Attacks and Special Attacks but they also share the weaknesses of both types. To explore the weaknesses and the correlating effectiveness, please check out the Types tables via the link in the top-left corner.

If a Pokemon's HP reaches 0 or they exhaust all of their charges for Regular Attacks and Special Attacks then they will be KO'd. The first player to have all of their Pokemon KO'd, loses.
